Most CKAD prep advice focuses on syntax. The exam actually tests speed and muscle memory more
than knowledge — you know the answer, the question is whether you can produce it in under three
minutes with `kubectl` and an editor.

## Where I lost the most practice-exam time

Multi-container pod patterns (sidecar, adapter, ambassador) and readiness/liveness probes were
the two areas where I knew the concept but was slow to write it correctly from memory.

## The drill that helped

I stopped reading and started timing myself: pick a scenario, set a five-minute timer, write the
manifest from scratch with no reference. Reviewing *why* I was slow mattered more than reviewing
whether the answer was right.
